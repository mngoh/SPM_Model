Soccer Player Model

Capstone Project: Player-Level Goals Prediction Model — Martin Ngoh

Problem Statement

With the increasing quantity of player transfers and high fees, clubs must make the right choice when choosing forwards. There is little room for error when deciding on who to recruit — a mistake can cost millions. Through this Player-Level Prediction model, clubs can reduce time and money spent on searching for players.

Key Findings

Linear models win

Linear Regression and Elastic Net tied for best performance (R²=0.680, MAE=0.198), outperforming all tree-based and deep learning approaches on this dataset.

Strong predictive signal

Player statistics explain 68% of variance in non-penalty goals scored. The model predicts within 0.198 goals on average.

Ensemble models underperform

Random Forest was the weakest model (R²=0.438). Tree-based ensembles may be overfitting or struggling with the log-transformed, PCA-reduced feature space.

Neural network convergence

5-layer network (50→40→30→20→10) with early stopping converged at epoch 28 of 1000. Test MSE=0.0854, suggesting diminishing returns over linear approaches.

Data reduction

Raw dataset reduced from 21,863 records to 2,786 unique players, then filtered to 829 players with more than 1 non-penalty goal for modelling.

Cross-validation stability

Linear Regression 5-fold CV: Mean RMSE 0.279 ± 0.015 — low variance confirms the model generalises well and isn't overfitting.
